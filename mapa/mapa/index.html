    INFRASTRUCTURE OF URBAN NON-HUMAN LIFE

    A radial map exploring how urban pigeons and microorganisms experience the city through environmental pressures, biological responses, and human relationships. This framework connects visible and invisible life to reveal multispecies injustice.

    CORE DICHOTOMY

    One species is visible but rejected (pigeons), one is invisible but essential (microorganisms). Together they expose how cities prioritize human aesthetics over ecological function.
